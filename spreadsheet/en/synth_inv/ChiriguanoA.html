Summary

We assume five processes that result in nasal-oral alternations: (1) leftward spreading LDNH triggered by nasal vowels and nasal stops, affecting all vowels and sonorants within the root and a potential series of prefixes; (2) LO, whereby /N/ --> [ND] / _V; (3) morphophonological LN, whereby /T/ --> [ND] / mõ+_; (4) Morphophonological syllabic LN whereby 3 specified suffixes (-ramo, -rani, -ma) nasalize the preceding root-final vowel; (5) A morphophonological process whereby two specific suffixes (-ɲo, -rã) initiate LDNH (as opposed to a root-internal trigger).

Notes

Our analysis differs from that of the original sources in several important ways, so it is worthwhile to justify these differences. Most importantly, we view the alternations between post-oralized and nasal stops as reflecting local oralization of underlyingly nasal stops by neighboring oral vowels (N -> ND / _V). The sources, however, view this alternation as reflective of nasalization of underlying oral or underspecified stops, either through LDNH (Dietrich) or through spontaneous nasalization (Daviet). In our view, the underlying nasal analysis better captures the data, because these segments can also be triggers of nasal harmony, as both sources record (LDNH:ND in Dietrich, LDNH in Daviet). This requires either some sort of spontaneous nasalization of the stop to feed further LDNH, or for nasal features to somehow be inserted, neither of which accord with the facts or with common assumptions in phonological theory. A simpler explanation is that these stops are in fact underlyingly nasal. Thus, they have nasal features from the get go which they can spread leftward through LDNH. The postoralized stops are explained as allomorphs of nasal stops that occur preceding underlying oral vowels when these fail to get nasalized through LDNH by a root nasal vowel. We are somewhat uncertain about the accuracy of MPP=LDNH:Root as it seems unlikely that two specified suffixes would initiate nasal harmony. It is possible that this is instead an LN or syllabic LN process whereby only the previous vowel is nasalized, however, Dietrich specifically includes examples of this process transcribed to demonstrate long distance nasalization. The alternation between mbo ~ mõ of the causative prefix follows the general pattern of LDNH.
