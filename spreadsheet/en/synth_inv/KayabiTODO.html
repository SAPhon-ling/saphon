Summary

We generally followed the analysis of Lapierre (2021). The distinguishing feature of the Kapiwat dialect is the presence of LDNH processes, which is absent in the other varieties. Beyond the LDNH processes, this dialect is virtually identical to the Jawarum dialect. Since the voiceless consonants are all transparent the the leftward LDNH process, we construed that the leftward LDNH process sees no opaque segments, an extrapolation based on the fact that voiced segments are rarely opaque to the spreading of nasality in Tupian languages in general.
