Summary

We base this synthesis mainly on Nicholson (1975/2009) and Harrison (1975), which are the two previous works with most substantive empirical engagement with the language. Asuriní does not exhibit alternation of [N]~[ND]~[D] type, not does it have contrastive nasal vowels. However, it does exhibit a number of morphophonological alternations affecting some consonants that undergo oralization or nasalization in the presence of certain affixes or lexically specified roots. Following Harrison we posit: (1) an LO process by which root-final /m, n, ŋ/ surface [p, t, k] in certain verb roots before the suffixes -a and -ta; (2) LO - {m, n} go to {w, ɾ} in certain roots; (3) LO - {m, n, ŋ} go to {p, h, k} in certain roots. We sided with Nicholson in the oralization of root-final /m, n/ into [w, ɾ], because we found it implausible that Nicholson would fail to notice an obvious natural group of vowel-initial suffixes. Following Harrison: (1) LO /N/ --> [T] before -ta; (2) LN /T/ --> [N] after mo-; (3) LO /n/ --> [w] after mo-. Asuriní lost contrastive nasal vowels, and therefore LDNH.
