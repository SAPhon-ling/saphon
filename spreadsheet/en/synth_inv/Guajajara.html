Summary

We mostly base this analysis on Bendor-Samuel's primary analysis, which includes an LN process whereby /T/ --> [N] after some lexically-specified roots, and add some of Silva's proposal for an LN process whereby Vs are nasalized to the right of a nasal consonant.

Notes

Guajajara lost contrastive nasal vowels, and therefore LDNH. However, the process of /T/ --> [ND] that is common at root-root or root-suffix boundaries in many of the Subgroup I TG languages is observed as a /T/ --> [N] alternation in Guajajara following those roots that had final nasal vowels. Since the nasal vowels are no longer present, the conditioning environment for this process is now lexically-specified by particular roots. The mrphn process that affects /puku, pe, kwer/ has been described by Soares and Bendor-Samuel as being a morphological process that affects a particular set of morphemes, triggered by a lexically-conditioned set of morphemes. Here, we have chosen to capture the fact that all of the undergoing morphemes have initial labial obstruents, namely /p, kw/, and to list the undergoers of the process as this natural class, within a particular set of morphemes. This captures the tendency observed elsewhere in TG for /p/ and /kw/ initial suffixes (but not /t, tS, k/ initial suffixes) to undergo nasalization.
