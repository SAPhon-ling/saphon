Citation

Abrahamson, Arne & Joyce Abrahamson. 1984. Os fonemas da língua júma. In Robert A. Dooley (ed.), Estudos sobre línguas tupí do Brasil, 157-174. Brasília: Summer Institute of Linguistics. Abrahamson, Arne & Joyce Abrahamson. 1980. Tentative phonemic statement for Júma. SIL Brasil.

Summary

This summary combines Abrahamson and Abrahamson's 1980 and 1984 analyses, given their similarity. The 1984 paper is a translation into Portuguese done by Eunice Burgess. Abrahamson & Abrahamson posit underlying N and contrastive nasal vowels, as well as one voiced stop for Júma and 5 phonological processes: (1) leftward LO, N post-oralizes before an oral vowel. The authors consider these allophones to be "pre-nasalized plosives" (1984:3) and proposes that this process is one of local nasalization. Given the oral vowel environment, it is most likely that this process is one that results in post-oralized allophones and that this is a local oralization process as opposed to one of LN, and therefore has been included as such; (2) rightward LO, shielding of /N/ to [DN] in syllable codas following an oral vowel (1984:3). The authors also describe these allophones as "post-nasalized plosives" but this seems to be a local oralization process; (3) leftward LN, /j/ nasalizes to [ɲ] preceding a nasal vowel (1984:4); (4) leftward LN, underlying N or their pre-nasalized ND allophones nasalize preceding vowels (1984:10); (5) leftward LN, the past suffix {-mɨ̃} and the future suffix {-nɛ̃hɛ} nasalize the final oral vowel of the preceding morpheme (1980:22). The nasalization of the morpheme-final oral vowel does not trigger the de-post-oralization of the preceding ND to N, such as in [ɔ̃mõmbɨ̃nɛ̃hɛ] 'will fasten' (1980:22). Nasal vowels can contrast in any position in the word, unlike most TG languages.
