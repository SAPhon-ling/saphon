Citation

Santos, Yonara Cristina de Souza dos. 2015. Fonética e fonologia preliminar da língua omágua/kambeba. UFAM:MA Thesis.

Summary

dos Santos posits underlying nasal consonants /m, n, ɲ/ and two contrastive nasal vowels /ĩ, ã/ for Omagua along with one phonological process: (1) leftward LN, /e/ and /u/ optionally nasalize to [ẽ] and [ũ], respectively, when followed by a nasal consonant. The author claims that these nasalized allophones occur in free variation with their oral counterparts (p. 55-56). We suspect that all vowels can be nasalized when they precede a nasal vowel, and that with a larger corpus, the author would have likely found this to be true. The author also notes the following: "Os fones vocálicos nasais, tem, em sua maioria, ocorrência condicionada às consoantes nasais." That said, only /ĩ, ã/ are observed in the author's data as nasal vowels not adjacent to a nasal consonant--again, we suspect there may be more phonemic nasal vowels, which the author may have uncovered with a larger lexical corpus, but we await further data to support this hypothesis. The author notes that [d] is restricted in the lexicon, and for this reason doesn't propose its status as phonemic. That said, the distribution of [d] closely parallels that of [b, g], and for this reason, we suspect that /d/ may likewise be contrastive (p. 40-41).

Notes

"O fone oclusivo alveolar [d], oclusivo glotal [ʔ], fricativos labiodentais [f] e [v], fricativo pós-alveolar [tʃ] oclusivo glotal [h] e o fone lateral alveolar [l] ocorrem com pouca frequência na língua." The author transcribes [l, v, h] in the data but does not include any phonemic or phonetic information regarding their occurence in Omagua. dos Santos notes the following regarding data collection: "Nesse sentido, esta dissertação objetiva descrever aspectos da fonologia da língua Omágua/Kambeba (família Tupi-Guarani) falada por indígenas que habitam o estado do Amazonas em aldeias no Rio Solimões e no Rio Negro, mais especificamente a língua falada na aldeia Tururukari-Uka (que significa “casa de Deus”) no município de Manacapuru e que assim se autoafirmam pertencentes a etnia Omágua/Kambeba (p. 14). Neste trabalho, utilizaremos “Omágua/Kambeba”, pois os indígenas referem-se a sua etnia ora como Omágua ora como Kambeba."
