Citation

Padua, Alexandre Jorge. 2007. Contribuição para a fonologia da língua Apiaká (Tupí-Guaraní). Universidade de Brasília: MA Thesis.

Summary

Padua proposes underlying nasal consonants and contrastive nasal vowels in Apiaká and 5 phonological processes: (1) leftward LN, /j/ is in free variation with [ɲ] before a nasal vowel. (p.28-29); (2) leftward LO, /m, ŋ/ post-oralize to [mb, ŋɡ] preceding an oral vowel in a stressed syllable (p.29-31); (3) leftward LO, /n/ post-oralizes to [nd] between any vowel and a stressed or pre-tonic vowel (p.30). This process is considered a separate one from the previous LO process because of Padua's description and analysis; (4) leftward LO, /N/ oralizes to [D] "after silence" before an oral vowel. The author claims that this process happens to /N/ "following silence" and applies to all voiced obstruents that appear following silence, /m, n, ŋ/ to [b, d, ɡ] (p.28-31); (5) circumdirectional LO, /ŋ/ oralizes to [ɡ] between two oral vowels.

Citation

Ehrenreich, Paul. 1895. Materialien zur Sprachenkunde Brasiliens: V. Die Sprache der Apiaka (Para). Zeitschrift für Ethnologie, 27.168-176

Summary

No description of harmony processes other than 'nasalization is common' (p. 171). Ehrenreich only proposes underlying nasal consonants and oral vowels, but no contrastive nasal vowels.

Notes

Ehrenreich doesn't use IPA, so some of the natural classes may be incorrect. indicates a 'cerebral' sound, which might be retroflex /ʈ/. <ṅ> is considered to be /ŋ/, while <χ> to be /χ/. might be /ɨ/, <ä> might be /ɛ/, and might be /ə/.
